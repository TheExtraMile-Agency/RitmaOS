# RitmaOS — Target Customer

## Initial target

Small service businesses and small agencies that already feel the pain of repeated knowledge work.

Good early targets:

- AI automation agencies
- Web/design agencies
- Marketing agencies
- Property management businesses
- Local service businesses
- Coaches/consultants
- Clinics and appointment-based businesses
- Youth sports/program operators

## Best-fit customer traits

The best early customer:

- Has repeatable workflows
- Uses documents, emails, client notes, proposals, or SOPs
- Wants AI but does not know how to organize it
- Repeats the same explanations often
- Has business-specific knowledge that generic AI does not know
- Needs quality control before sending work to clients
- Is willing to pay for setup, clarity, and time savings

## Poor-fit customers

Avoid early customers who:

- Want magic automation with no involvement
- Have no repeatable process
- Refuse to document anything
- Need heavy enterprise security on day one
- Need fully autonomous agents immediately
- Are only looking for cheap prompt packs

## Buyer pain

The buyer says things like:

- “We tried AI but it is all over the place.”
- “I keep explaining the same context again.”
- “My team uses ChatGPT differently and nothing is standardized.”
- “We need SOPs but never have time to write them.”
- “I want AI to help, but I do not trust the output.”
- “We have too many tools and no system.”

## Initial wedge

The simplest wedge is a done-for-you AI workspace setup.

Offer:

“We will turn one of your repeatable business workflows into a structured AI workspace your team can actually use.”
